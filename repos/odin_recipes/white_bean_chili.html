Tuna Casserole

Read along for my take on a weeknight classic

This is a classic go to casserole for the days when time is scarce, you have a fridge full of...whatever, and you just don't want to think too much. I promise this will knock your' socks off and become a new family favorite. Even for the pickiest of eaters.

Here is what you will need

  • 2 Cans Tuna (I prefer Solid White, although the brand and type is not altogether important
  • 1 16oz bag of spirl egg noodles
  • 1 12oz can of Campbells* Cream of Mushroom Soup
  • 1 Tbsp Black Pepper
  • 1 Tsp Coarse Sea Saly
  • 1 16oz bag of shredded jack cheese (Substitute and alternative if you would like)

Here's how to make it

  1. Pre-heat the oven to 375
  2. Set a pot with 8qt water to high. Bring to a boil
  3. Add salt to the water as it is coming up to temp
  4. Open and drain cans of tuna, add to a large mixing bowl
  5. Add half the bag of cheese, the can of soup, and the pepper to the bowl with the tuna
  6. Once the water is at boiling temperature, add the noodles. Boil for 10 minutes, or until al dente
  7. When the noodles are cooked through, drain the water and add half of the contents to a large baking dish
  8. layer the soup mixture to the noodles, then repeat. Once complete, sprinkle remaining cheese over the top
  9. Bake at 375 for 23 minutes
  10. Let it cool, serve, then Enjoy!
